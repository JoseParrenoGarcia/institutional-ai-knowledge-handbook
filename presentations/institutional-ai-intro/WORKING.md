# Working Instructions — Institutional AI Presentation

## Context

This folder captures the development of a lightning talk (~10 minutes) for a data science discipline of ~60 people, including managers. The audience has no prior exposure to institutional AI as a concept.

The speaker's goal is to shift the mental model of the discipline from "documentation for humans" to "information architecture for humans and agents". The core vision: knowledge that was previously trapped in individual heads or inaccessible formats becomes a compounding asset — structured, navigable, and usable by both people and AI agents.

---

## Presentation Brief

- **Audience:** ~60 data scientists and managers, mixed seniority
- **Format:** Lightning talk, ~10 minutes (not yet committed to slide deck vs. doc)
- **Tone:** Mix — open with vision, close with concrete near-term actions
- **Current stage:** Brainstorming — content and flow, not final format

---

## How We Work

### Iteration process

- All content lives in markdown files in this folder first
- `brainstorm.md` is the working document for content and narrative flow
- Use section markers to flag ideas that are uncertain: `> **[DRAFT]** ...`
- When the brainstorm is stable, we'll move to a structured outline (`outline.md`), then slides if needed

### Naming convention

- `brainstorm.md` — current working content
- `outline.md` — structured narrative ready for slide conversion (created later)
- `slides/` — slide content per slide when we move to production (created later)

### When asked to iterate on the presentation

1. Read `brainstorm.md` in full before suggesting changes
2. Respect the 10-minute constraint — do not inflate scope
3. Suggest changes as edits to the markdown, not as separate docs
4. When the user approves a section, mark it `> **[LOCKED]**` to signal it's stable

---

## What NOT to Do

- Do not add jargon-heavy AI/ML language — this audience is technical but the framing must be accessible to managers too
- Do not over-engineer the structure — this is a talk, not a whitepaper
- Do not invent statistics or cite sources without flagging them as unverified
- Do not skip the "why this matters to me personally" angle — the talk needs a human anchor
- Do not suggest adding more than 10 slides — the 10-minute limit is hard
- Do not make the future vision abstract or utopian — ground it in recognisable work scenarios

---

## Guiding Principles for the Content

- **Agents first, not agents only** — humans remain central; agents multiply what humans can do
- **Information, not documentation** — the mindset shift is from writing docs for humans to designing information that works for both
- **Compounding, not replacing** — 1 person depositing structured knowledge → N people and agents benefit
- **Three levels** — the talk covers project, team, and discipline scope
- **Short-term goals must be achievable** — not a transformation roadmap, just 3 concrete first steps
